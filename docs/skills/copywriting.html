Copywriting

The most transferable skill on the internet. Words that sell work everywhere — ads, emails, landing pages, sales calls.

$65k–$120k/yr employed · $50–$250/hr freelance · First client possible by Day 30

Why this skill

Copywriting is the only skill on this list that directly makes sales. Not indirectly — directly. You write words, those words generate revenue, and businesses will pay well for that. Every business that sells anything needs copy: landing pages, emails, ads, product descriptions, sales decks. The demand is everywhere.

The equity path is real too. Good copywriters build audiences by sharing their work publicly. That audience becomes an asset — newsletter sponsorships, consulting clients, courses, and SaaS tools built on your own audience.

How it becomes equity

Content

Post your copy breakdowns publicly. The people who follow "copywriters who teach" become your inbound client pipeline.

Code

Build a productized copywriting service or a swipe file tool. Package what you know into something that sells itself.

Capital

A copywriter who understands consumer psychology can spot undervalued businesses and either consult or invest early.

The 90-day path

Days 1–30
Foundation

Psychology of persuasion, AIDA, headlines, hooks. Reading great copy daily. Writing every day.

Days 31–60
Application

Long-form sales copy, email sequences, landing pages. Auditing real brands. Building your swipe file.

Days 61–90
Monetization

Portfolio, cold outreach, first paid project. Targeting small businesses, newsletters, or SaaS companies.
