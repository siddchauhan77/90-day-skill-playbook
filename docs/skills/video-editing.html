Video Editing

Every creator, brand, and business needs video. You can be the person who makes it good.

$50k–$100k/yr employed · $40–$150/hr freelance · First client possible by Day 45

Why this skill

YouTube alone has created an insane demand for skilled editors. Every serious creator needs one — and the moment they can afford to outsource it, they do. That's your opening. Add short-form (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) and the demand has tripled in the last three years. The bar for quality is rising and the volume of content is exploding.

The strategic play: edit for a creator who's growing. Negotiate rev share early. Shaan's MrBeast example — being the editor of a growing channel is worth equity, not just a paycheck.

Shaan's framework mapping

Primary

You take raw footage and produce a finished product — a video that didn't exist before you touched it.

How it becomes equity

Content

Build your own YouTube channel around the editing process — tutorials, breakdowns, and behind-the-scenes. That audience becomes your inbound client pipeline.

Code

LUT packs, templates, preset stores on Gumroad. Make once, sell forever. Productized editing services at fixed price packages.

Capital

Partner with creators early for equity or rev share instead of a flat fee. Be the editor of the next channel that blows up.

The 90-day path

Days 1–30
Foundation

DaVinci Resolve basics, cuts, transitions, your first complete edit. Understanding the timeline, color basics, and audio.

Days 31–60
Application

Color grading, sound design, music sync. YouTube-style long-form edits. Short-form Reels and TikTok cuts.

Days 61–90
Monetization

Portfolio reel, cold outreach to creators, first paid gig. Target small YouTubers and podcast editors first.

How to sell this skill

The pitch, the proof, what you hand over, and what the client actually measures.

Your pitch

"Send me your raw footage. I'll send back a finished edit — captions, music, color grade — in 48 hours. First video is free so you can see the quality."

Proof that closes
  • 15-second before/after clip (raw vs. finished)
  • Portfolio reel: 60–90 seconds of your best cuts
  • Retention graph from a video you edited (screenshot)
  • 3 finished videos across different styles/formats
What you deliver
  • Finished video file (H.264, platform-spec)
  • SRT caption file
  • Thumbnail (if in scope)
  • Short-form cut for Reels/Shorts (if agreed)
  • Project file on request
Outcomes to promise
  • Average view duration / retention %
  • Watch time per video
  • Subscriber growth rate after editing
  • Engagement rate (comments + likes)
  • Creator's time saved per video
