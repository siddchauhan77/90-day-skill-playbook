Video Editing

Every creator, brand, and business needs video. You can be the person who makes it good.

$50k–$100k/yr employed · $40–$150/hr freelance · First client possible by Day 45

Why this skill

YouTube alone has created an insane demand for skilled editors. Every serious creator needs one — and the moment they can afford to outsource it, they do. That's your opening. Add short-form (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) and the demand has tripled in the last three years. The bar for quality is rising and the volume of content is exploding.

The strategic play: edit for a creator who's growing. Negotiate rev share early. Shaan's MrBeast example — being the editor of a growing channel is worth equity, not just a paycheck.

How to sell this skill

The pitch, the proof, what you hand over, and what the client actually measures.

Your pitch

"Send me your raw footage. I'll send back a finished edit — captions, music, color grade — in 48 hours. First video is free so you can see the quality."

Proof that closes
  • 15-second before/after clip (raw vs. finished)
  • Portfolio reel: 60–90 seconds of your best cuts
  • Retention graph from a video you edited (screenshot)
  • 3 finished videos across different styles/formats
What you deliver
  • Finished video file (H.264, platform-spec)
  • SRT caption file
  • Thumbnail (if in scope)
  • Short-form cut for Reels/Shorts (if agreed)
  • Project file on request
Outcomes to promise
Only things you control
  • Edit delivered within 48–72hr turnaround
  • 2 revision rounds per video
  • Captions, color grade, and transitions per brand guide
  • Exported in all required formats (YouTube, Reels, TikTok)
  • Project files delivered on request

Fastest path to first client

No cold blast. No waiting 60 days. Here's the exact sequence to land client #1.

First client in ~3–7 days
  1. Day 1: DM 5 YouTubers in your niche with 1k–50k subscribers. Offer to edit their next video for free as a paid test if they like it. Creators are always looking — the bottleneck is finding reliable editors, not budget.
  2. Days 2–3: Join creator Discord servers and Twitter/X creator communities. Many post "editor needed" every week. Search "looking for editor" on Twitter and respond same day.
  3. Days 4–5: Post a before/after edit — a raw clip vs. your edited version — on Twitter, TikTok, or LinkedIn. Make the edit itself the pitch. Show your style in 60 seconds.
  4. Days 6–7: Apply to the Creator Wizard newsletter job board and VideoEditorJobs.co. These have real postings from paid creators who already budget for editors.
⚡ Do NOT pitch big channels first. Start with 1k–50k subs — they respond faster, give more creative control, and often become long-term retainer clients.

Build for yourself first

Do the thing for yourself before you sell it to others. The output becomes your proof of work.

Start your own channel or account

Edit your own content — even talking-head or screen recordings. Your retention graphs are your portfolio. Post consistently for 30 days.

Proof → Retention analytics screenshot

Create a before/after reel

Take free stock footage from Pexels/Mixkit. Edit raw vs. finished. 60 seconds. Post it. Most editors never do this.

Proof → Public reel post

Edit breakdown post

Take any of your edits and explain every cut decision. Why this music, why this pacing. Shows taste, not just technical skill.

Proof → Content that builds trust

Build a short-form repurposing pipeline

Use CapCut + Claude to auto-cut long video into clips. Document the workflow. Sell the system, not just the edits.

Proof → Workflow doc + demo
