## Shaan's Framework: Which Categories This Hits

| Category | How Video Editing Fits |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Making** (primary) | You create a finished product — edited video — from raw footage |
| **Designing** (secondary) | Great editing requires taste: pacing, color, motion, visual storytelling |

This is a **Making** skill with a strong design component. The people who command premium rates aren't just cutting clips — they're shaping how the story feels.

---

## Why YouTube Created a Gold Rush

YouTube alone has created an insane demand for skilled editors. The MrBeast effect means:

- Every serious creator needs an editor
- The bar for quality is rising (audiences expect cinematic edits)
- Volume is up (weekly uploads across millions of channels)
- Short-form (Reels, TikTok, Shorts) has tripled the demand

Most creators start editing themselves. The moment they can afford to outsource it, they do. That's your opening.

## DaVinci Resolve vs Premiere Pro

| Tool | Cost | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| **DaVinci Resolve** | Free | Color grading, narrative, cinematic |
| **Premiere Pro** | ~$55/month | Most agencies, corporate, YouTube |
| **CapCut** | Free | Short-form, Reels, TikTok |

**Start with DaVinci Resolve.** It's free, industry-grade, and the color tools are the best in the business.

## What $150/Hour Editors Do Differently

1. They understand story, not just software
2. Their cuts have rhythm — they feel the music and the emotion
3. They deliver on time, every time (reliability is rare)
4. They communicate well with creators about vision
5. They know color theory and can make footage look intentional
