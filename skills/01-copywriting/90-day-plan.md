# Copywriting: 90-Day Plan

## Phase Overview
- Phase 1 — Foundation (Days 1–30): Psychology of persuasion, frameworks (AIDA/PAS/4U), headlines, daily rewrites
- Phase 2 — Application (Days 31–60): Long-form copy, short-form ads, email sequences, critique real copy
- Phase 3 — Monetization (Days 61–90): Portfolio build, cold outreach, land first paid project

> One rule: show up every day. Post at least one thing. The practice compounds.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Days 1–30)

### Day 1 — Why Copywriting Works: The Psychology of Desire

**Task:** Read chapters 1–3 of *The Boron Letters* by Gary Halbert (free online). Write a 200-word summary of the core idea in your own words: what does copy actually do in the mind of the reader?

**Posts:**
1. Hook: "I started learning copywriting today. Here's the first thing that broke my brain:" — Angle: Share the insight that copy isn't about writing, it's about understanding what people already want and connecting it to your offer.
2. Hook: "Most people think good copy = clever writing. Wrong." — Angle: Explain the difference between entertaining writing and persuasive writing — one makes people think, the other makes people act.
3. Hook: "Day 1 of learning copywriting. The first lesson: nobody cares about you or your product." — Angle: The core insight that all great copy starts with the reader's existing desires, fears, and frustrations — not with the seller's features. *(optional)*

3. Hook: "I wrote a weight loss ad today using PAS. The agitate section took 45 minutes to get right." — Angle: Share what makes agitation hard — it's not about being mean, it's about being specific about the exact emotional experience of the problem. *(optional)*
